Break Down of Variables

  • Inverse dependent variables, not included together in a regression to avoid multicollinearity, each will run as a separate outcome model.

    • Abortion_Rate - per 1000 women age 15-44, expected to decrease post-Dobbs

    • Birth_Rate - per 1000 women age 15-44, expected to increase post-Dobbs

  • Telehealth_share

    • share of total abortion completed via telehealth, monthly, state-level. women in ban states maintaining partial access to abortion through shield law states.

  • Medicaid_abortion_funding_restrictions

    • 1 - state restricts Medicaid funding for abortion

    • 0 - otherwise

  • Medicaid_expansion_status

    • 1 - state expanded Medicaid under ACA

    • 0 - otherwise

  • Shield_law_state

    • 1 - state enacted a shield law protecting abortion providers from out-of-state prosecution

    • 0 - otherwise

  • Shield_law_access

    • 1 - state shield law explicity protects patient access to out-of-state Telehealth abortion services

    • 0 - otherwise

  • Gestational_limit_weeks

    • Showing point in pregnancy each state prohibits abortion (0 - total ban, 6 week, 15 week, 20 week, 24 week)

  • Single_mothers_median_monthly_income

    • will most likely be dropped as it was used to figure stats for other variables to avoid multicollinearity.

  • Infant_childcare_cost_burden

    • percentage of income spent on childcare as a share of single mothers median monthly income.

  • SNAP_participation_rate

    • share of the total population enrolled in SNAP

  • TANF_caseloads_per_1000_women

    • single parent TANF caseloads per 1000 women aged 15-44

  • State_unemployment_rate

    • monthly state-level unemployment rate

  • Minimum_wage

    • state level minimum wage, updated on known legislative change dates

  • CPI

    • monthly CPI to control for inflation trends

  • RPP_all_items

    • Regional Price Partities, controls for structural cost of living differences across states

  • Census_region

    • categorical variable identifying each state’s Census region (Northeast, South, Midwest, West) used to assign appropriate region CPI data.

BVI Findings:

  • Restrictive States (Green) show a drop in abortion rates per 1000 women by June 2022. There is a steady pace until 10 months onward after Dobbs took effect. Most likely due to women adapting to the policy change (Telehealth medication abortions, traveling to another state with less restrictions).

  • Shield Law States (Blue) show a spike right after the Dobbs policy change took place. This also shows a spill over effect. Like noted above, women travel from other states to receive their care.

  • Middle Ground States (Red) - Before Dobbs, middle ground states were pretty consistent with 1 abortion per 1000 women 15-44. After Dobbs it drifts upwards, which is most likely from women in neighboring restrictive states crossing over for care.

  • Blue (Shield Law States) - Started with the highest SNAP participation before Dobbs then dropped steadily throughout the post-period, likely reflecting the fact that shield law states tend to be wealthier states that were already experiencing declining SNAP enrollment independent of abortion policy
